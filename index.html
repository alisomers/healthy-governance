Future-Proof Healthcare Through AI Governance

Introduction

Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming healthcare. Adaptive, lifecycle-based governance is critical to ensuring that patient safety, equity, and system resilience are protected as AI adoption accelerates.

The Problem

Existing policies often lag behind the dynamic, iterative development of AI systems. Static regulations cannot accommodate continuous learning algorithms, evolving data models, or shifting clinical contexts, leaving critical gaps in patient protections and liability frameworks.

The Need

Healthcare systems require governance models that are flexible, risk-tiered, and lifecycle-aligned, ensuring oversight from AI conception through to decommissioning. Without this, both patients and providers face increased risk of harm, inequity, and distrust.

The Opportunity

Canada has an opportunity to lead globally by adopting an Adaptive AI Governance Framework in healthcare that strengthens innovation, protects patient rights, and fosters ethical AI adoption at scale.

Call to Action

Healthcare organizations, regulators, and developers must work together to implement dynamic, participatory AI governance frameworks anchored in equity, safety, and transparency. Join the movement to future-proof healthcare through adaptive governance.
